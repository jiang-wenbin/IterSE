Iterative Self-Supervised Speech Enhancement without Clean Speech Online Supplement

Authors

Wenbin Jiang, Jin Li, Xuanhao Cai, Fei Wen, Kai Yu

Abstract

Most deep neural network based speech enhancement methods usually train the model in a supervised manner, which requires massive noisy-to-clean data pairs for training. However, due to the complexity and diversity of realistic environments, it is difÔ¨Åcult or even impractical to collect the ground-truth clean speech in some applications. To address this problem, we propose a novel self-supervised learning method for speech enhancement that does not require clean reference speech. Specifically, in the first training stage, we use the unprocessed noisy speech and noise to construct noisier-to-noisy data pairs to train an initial model. Then, in the second training stage, we iteratively construct sampled noisier-to-noisy data pairs based on the model learned in the previous epoch to train a better model. Experimental results on the popular benchmark datasets show that the proposed method achieves competitive performance with the supervised learning method.
